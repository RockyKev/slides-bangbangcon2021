NetMonsters was free downloadable game from the late 90s. The player's job was to collect monsters and... (this is straight from the guide)

stop Shade and his evil Minions from destroying websites, clogging up Phone Lines, and generaally making the Internet an unpleasant place to surf.

The player would download the NetMonster client, grab a special URL, and with that URL, the client would generate a monster that you try to capture. 1 monster, 1 web Page.

(64 words)

netmonsters lasted from 1999-2003. Even though the game has been abandoned for more than 15 years... you can still run the NetMonster client today! It was written in .net, and works on Windows 10 machines! Absolute Tech marvel.

The game stopped at version 1.4, because a hard drive crash erased the source code. And there was never a NetMonster V2.

(46 words) With the source code lost -- we don't know how exactly monsters get generated any more. So in order to talk hypothetically about lost code, I want to present a analog to NetMonster, the game that inspired it... the Monster Rancher Series. (And really Monster Rancher 2)
(54 words) In the Monster Rancher Series-- The way Monster Rancher worked was that you would insert a CD, and based on the Table of Contents data, that would determine what kind of monster would be generated. It would go through a bunch of conditionals, decide the type of monster, the variant, and some other attributes.

(89 words)

For Netmonster, rather than CDs... it was URLs. Similar process.

  • The player would grab a URL with a NetMonster badge.
  • The game Client would look for the badge within the URL.
  • Based on that code snippet, the game client would generate a monster.

Collecting was a huge part of NetMonster. You need strong monsters to defeat the bad guys. And the early internet was different. Today, we cluster around massive community sites like Reddit and Facebook or specialized Wikis.

In the early internet, back when it was called information superhighway... There were no wikipedia!

Instead, ambitious techie types would claim a piece of the internet for themselves with free website builders like Geocities, or Tripod or the dozen other free web hosts.Google was still being figured out.

And much of the internet instead was organized into neigherhoods of like-minded topics, often in what we called webrings. People made websites based on a specific topic.

Really, very often... we just created shrines for your anime waifu or your pop-star snacku... and really let people know what we liked.

And this is a round-about way of saying... If you wanted to win at NetMonsters, you needed to be involved in the NetMonsters community, which means visiting the Netmonsters webrings, which means being familiar with the NetMonsters fansites.
