(Show next monsters...) The monsters came in all sorts of weird shapes and sizes... The tagline of the game was -- Internet Monster Hunting at it's finest!

(83 words)

NetMonsters was free downloadable game from the late 90s, where you collect monsters from URLs.

For Netmonster, it was very Pokemon-esque with collecting. How it worked was:

  • The player would grab a URL with a NetMonster badge.
  • The player would enter it in the game.
  • Based on the code snippet, the game client would generate a monster.
  • 1 monster. 1 URL.

You need strong monsters to defeat the bad guys.

The game stopped at version 1.4, because a hard drive crash erased the source code. And there was never a NetMonster V2.

(64 words)

And here's a crazy fact. The game has been abandoned for more than 15 years... you can still run the NetMonster client today! It was written in .net, and works on Windows 10 machines! Absolute Tech marvel.

Today, we cluster around massive community sites like Reddit and Facebook or specialized Wikis.

In the early 2000s when we called it information superhighway... There was no Wikipedia!

But there was a lot of people who wanted to share their knowledge... so they built websites. And often, it was free! So there was a industry of free website builders like Geocities, or Tripod or a dozen other free web hosts.

To find these sites was still a challenge. Google was still being figured out in 2000. Searching existed. But good luck!

Much of the internet was organized into topics, in what we called webrings. People made websites based on a specific topic, and then join a web ring.

Really, very often... we just created shrines for our anime waifus. And you'd put your website on the webring. And now people can now find it.

And going back to NetMonsters -- There were NetMonster websites. Because to catch NetMonsters, you needed to find websites with the netmonster badge, which means visiting the Netmonsters webrings, which means being familiar with fansites about NetMonsters.
